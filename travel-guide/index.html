Wuxi Travel Guide

Essential Information for Your Trip to Wuxi

Plan Your Wuxi Trip

Everything you need to know before visiting Wuxi

Best Time to Visit

Wuxi has a temperate climate with four distinct seasons. The best time to visit Wuxi is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Spring (March-May)

Temperature: 10-20°C (50-68°F)

Highlights: Cherry blossoms in Uantouzhu, comfortable weather for sightseeing

Summer (June-August)

Temperature: 25-35°C (77-95°F)

Highlights: Taihu Lake water activities, lotus flowers

Autumn (September-November)

Temperature: 15-25°C (59-77°F)

Highlights: Pleasant weather, autumn foliage, harvest festivals

Winter (December-February)

Temperature: 0-10°C (32-50°F)

Highlights: Less crowded, traditional Spring Festival celebrations

Transportation Guide

Wuxi is well-connected by various modes of transportation, making it easy to reach and navigate within the city.

By Air

Wuxi Shuofang International Airport (WUX) is located about 12 kilometers from the city center. It offers domestic flights to major cities in China, as well as some international flights.

Transportation from the airport: Airport shuttle buses, taxis, and ride-hailing services are available.

By Train

Wuxi has two main railway stations: Wuxi Railway Station and Wuxi East Railway Station. Both offer high-speed train services to Shanghai, Nanjing, Hangzhou, and other major cities.

Travel time to Shanghai: Approximately 30 minutes by high-speed train.

Within the City

Public transportation: Wuxi has an extensive bus network and a metro system with multiple lines.

Taxi: Readily available throughout the city. Ride-hailing apps like Didi are also popular.

Bicycle: Shared bikes are available for short-distance travel.

Food Recommendations

Wuxi cuisine is known for its sweet and savory flavors, with a focus on fresh ingredients from Taihu Lake. Here are some must-try dishes:

Wuxi-style Xiaolongbao

Steamed dumplings filled with pork and broth, with a slightly sweet taste. Best enjoyed at local restaurants like Guihua Lou.

Braised Pork Ribs

Tender pork ribs cooked with soy sauce, sugar, and other seasonings, resulting in a sweet and savory flavor.

Silver Fish from Taihu Lake

Delicate small fish from Taihu Lake, often stir-fried or made into soup.

Osmanthus Cake

Traditional sweet cake made with rice flour, sugar, and osmanthus flowers, with a fragrant aroma.

Recommended dining areas: Nanchang Street, Huishan Ancient Town, and the area around Taihu Lake.

Travel Tips

Practical Tips

  • Language: Mandarin is the primary language, but some tourist areas may have English-speaking staff.
  • Currency: Chinese Yuan (CNY). Credit cards are accepted in major hotels and restaurants, but cash is recommended for smaller establishments.
  • Internet: Free Wi-Fi is available in many hotels and public areas. Consider getting a local SIM card for data access.
  • Weather: Check the weather forecast before your trip and pack accordingly.
  • Health: Drink bottled water and be cautious with street food if you have a sensitive stomach.

Sightseeing Tips

  • Plan your itinerary in advance, especially during peak seasons.
  • Book tickets online for popular attractions to avoid long lines.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes as there will be a lot of walking.
  • Consider hiring a guide for a more in-depth experience.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples and religious sites.

Recommended Itinerary

3-Day Itinerary:

  • Day 1: Uantouzhu Scenic Area, Taihu Lake
  • Day 2: Lingshan Giant Buddha Scenic Area, Huishan Ancient Town
  • Day 3: Nanchang Street, Wuxi Museum
