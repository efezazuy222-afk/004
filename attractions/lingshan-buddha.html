Lingshan Giant Buddha

One of the Largest Buddha Statues in the World

About Lingshan Giant Buddha

The Lingshan Giant Buddha is an 88-meter tall bronze statue of Maitreya Buddha located in the Lingshan Scenic Area, Wuxi, Jiangsu Province, China. Completed in 1996, it is one of the largest Buddha statues in the world and a major Buddhist pilgrimage site.

The statue is situated on the shores of Taihu Lake, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can climb the stairs to the base of the statue for a closer look and to experience the serene atmosphere of the area.

The Lingshan Scenic Area also includes other attractions such as the Brahma Palace, a magnificent Buddhist temple complex, and the Nine Dragons Fountain, which features a spectacular water and light show.

Key Facts

Height: 88 meters (289 feet)
Completion: 1996
Material: Bronze
Location: Lingshan Scenic Area, Wuxi
Best Time to Visit: Spring and Autumn

Visitor Information

The Lingshan Scenic Area is open daily from 7:30 AM to 5:30 PM. The entrance fee includes access to all attractions within the area, including the Giant Buddha, Brahma Palace, and Nine Dragons Fountain.

It is recommended to allocate at least half a day to fully explore the scenic area. Comfortable walking shoes are essential as there is a fair amount of walking involved, including climbing stairs to reach the base of the Buddha statue.

For a more immersive experience, consider hiring a guide who can provide detailed information about the history and significance of the site.
