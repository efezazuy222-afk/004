Huishan Ancient Town

A Well-Preserved Ancient Town with Rich Cultural Heritage

About Huishan Ancient Town

Huishan Ancient Town is a well-preserved ancient town located in the western part of Wuxi. With a history dating back over 3,000 years, it is one of the oldest towns in the region and a significant cultural heritage site.

The town features traditional Chinese architecture, including old houses, temples, and ancestral halls. It is surrounded by mountains and has a network of canals, giving it a typical Jiangnan water town atmosphere.

Visitors can explore the ancient streets, visit historical sites such as the Huishan Temple and the Jichang Garden, and experience traditional Chinese culture. The town is also known for its traditional crafts, such as pottery and bamboo weaving, and its local specialties, such as Huishan clay figurines.

Key Facts

Location: Western part of Wuxi
History: Over 3,000 years
Main Attractions: Huishan Temple, Jichang Garden, ancient streets
Famous For: Huishan clay figurines, traditional crafts
Best Time to Visit: Spring and Autumn

Visitor Information

Huishan Ancient Town is open daily from 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM. The entrance fee includes access to most attractions within the town.

One of the main attractions in the town is the Huishan Temple, which was built during the Eastern Jin Dynasty (317-420 AD) and is one of the oldest temples in Wuxi. Another popular site is the Jichang Garden, a classical Chinese garden built in the Ming Dynasty.

Visitors can also explore the ancient streets, which are lined with traditional shops and restaurants. Be sure to try some local specialties, such as Huishan clay figurines, which are a traditional craft unique to the area.

The town is easily accessible by public transportation, with several bus routes connecting it to the city center. It is recommended to allocate at least half a day to fully explore the town and its attractions.
