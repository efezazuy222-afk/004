Taihu Lake

The Third Largest Freshwater Lake in China

About Taihu Lake

Taihu Lake, also known as Lake Tai, is the third largest freshwater lake in China, located in the Yangtze River Delta. It covers an area of about 2,250 square kilometers and is shared by the provinces of Jiangsu and Zhejiang.

The lake is known for its beautiful scenery, with clear water, lush mountains, and numerous islands. It is often referred to as the "Pearl of the Yangtze River Delta" and is a popular tourist destination.

Wuxi is located on the northern shore of Taihu Lake, and the lake plays a significant role in the city's economy, culture, and tourism. Visitors can enjoy various water activities, such as boat rides, fishing, and swimming, as well as explore the many scenic spots around the lake.

Best Time to Visit: Spring and Autumn

Visitor Information

There are several scenic areas around Taihu Lake in Wuxi, including Uantouzhu, Lingshan Scenic Area, and Meiliang Lake. Each offers different views and activities.

Boat tours are a popular way to explore the lake, with various routes available that take visitors to different islands and scenic spots. Some tours include stops at places like Sanshan Island, which is known for its natural beauty and historical sites.

For those interested in water sports, there are opportunities for sailing, jet skiing, and paddleboarding in designated areas. Fishing is also permitted in certain parts of the lake.

Visitors can also enjoy the lake views from various parks and viewing platforms around Wuxi, such as the Taihu Lake National Wetland Park.
